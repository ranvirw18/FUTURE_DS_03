This project focuses on analyzing student perception data to evaluate teaching effectiveness across various departments. The primary objective is to monitor faculty performance against an institutional benchmark of 8.0/10.

The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of teaching scores, student participation, and specific category ratings, allowing educational administrators to identify strengths and areas requiring strategic intervention.

Participation Tracking: Total count of Student IDs (1,001) to ensure data significance.

Categorical Breakdown: A horizontal bar chart comparing metrics such as:

Course Recommendations

Degree of Difficulty

Explanation of Concepts

Subject Knowledge

Doubt Resolution

Quality Assurance Insights: A dedicated section for automated/manual key observations and recommendations.

üìà Key Insights from the Analysis
Benchmark Gap: The institution is currently performing at 5.96, which is significantly below the 8.0 target, suggesting a need for faculty training programs.

Mastery vs. Communication: Faculty show high scores in Subject Knowledge, but significantly lower scores in the Explanation of Concepts, indicating a gap in pedagogical delivery rather than technical expertise.

Practical Application: Metrics related to the practical application of theory consistently underperform, representing a critical area for curriculum improvement.
